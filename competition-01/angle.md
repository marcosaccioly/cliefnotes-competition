# Angle lock — Competition #01

This document locks the authorial angle before content. After here, we don't reopen the framing — only refine execution.

## Working name

**Voice as Presence**

(Final name can shift during Stage 6 Critic pass; the concept stays.)

## Why this angle

Intersection of three forces:

1. **Author fingerprint (Marquito):** design + edutech with a contemplative / philosophical strand. The natural angle is to treat voice as a *quality of attention*, not a list of adjectives.
2. **Client philosophy (Dana):** Ruff Cuts' reason to exist is **attentive presence that reduces stress** — for the dog, for the owner. The business is calm-expert-showing-up, not grooming-in-a-clinic. The voice must be consistent with the service.
3. **Community vote (Clief Notes, AI-first):** a conceptual frame defended with method is more memorable than yet another brand-voice template.

All three pull in the same direction. That is why the lock lands here.

## Thesis in one sentence

> A voice guide should not describe how the brand sounds — it should make the brand present.

## 4-layer structure

Guide organized by increasing depth, from "who" to "how AI reproduces":

1. **Core** — archetype / psychological base. One central concept, not an archetype wheel. Locked: **The Trusted Expert At Hand** — expertise that shows up, ready and close. Echoes the mobile van, the ex-vet-tech calm, and the "at your driveway" promise.
2. **Principles** — 4-5 operating principles in direct language (e.g., *"Respect the reader's time"*, *"Name the animal, not the category"*). Not 20 adjectives.
3. **Patterns** — concrete before / after pairs covering the 4 scenarios asked in the brief (caption, apology email, complaint handling, competitor mention) + booking confirmation / text-when-done as a 5th, because it is Ruff Cuts' signature operational moment.
4. **The System** — the transparent AI layer: a system prompt encoding everything above + few-shot examples derived from patterns + a self-check rubric to review outputs.

## Tactical differentiator

**Study mode overlay** on the site. In each section of the guide, a toggle reveals:

- *Why this choice* — editorial decision explained.
- *How the prompt enforces it* — snippet from the system prompt responsible for the behavior.
- *AI example — with / without* — before and after the rule is applied.

So the voter sees the guide working **and** learns to replicate the method. That is what differentiates from "yet another nice doc".

## Execution fingerprint (how it sounds / looks)

- **Guide copy:** speaks of *presence, attention, trust, respect* where competitors will speak of *engagement, reach, authentic, vibrant*.
- **Design:** serious typography (likely serif headings + sans body), clear hierarchy, generous whitespace. Calm, not loud.
- **Pedagogical register:** the guide *teaches the voice* while demonstrating it — every section is written in the very voice it prescribes. Meta-consistency = proof.

## Non-goals (reinforced from brief-decoded)

- No adjective soup.
- No mascot or named persona (e.g. "meet Sparky, our voice").
- No agency jargon (archetype wheel, voice matrix, tone spectrum slider).
- No hipster manifesto ("we believe in a world where...").
- No hiding the AI system — transparency is the product.

## Known risk

"Voice as Presence" can sound pretentious for a dog-grooming business. Mitigation: the guide's copy must be *ground-level, practical, concrete* — the concept is philosophical, the execution is operational. If the copy can't pull this off, we drop the framing at Critic pass (Stage 6) and reframe.
